Rose curves

What are rose curves like? Like putting a pen at the end of a wheel and spinning it to draw a circle. However, a wheel has a fixed distance between the center spoke and the wheel's edge but for a rose curve we change the diameter in an oscillating way.

Mathmatically, roses are a family of curves defined as \[ x = cos() cos() y = cos() sin() \] where n and k...

The rose equation is a variation on a circle. If I wanted to draw a circle, I would use the following equation \[ x = r cos(\theta) y = r sin(\theta) \] where r is the radius.

Because these curves are based on oscillating terms, they produce lovely circular patterns (repetitive patterns). Below are the results of various experiments made with processing.
